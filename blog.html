Cryptography is the art and science of securing communication.

Date: 8th July, 2024 Time: 10:00 AM Location: Malacca, Malaysia


Imagine you’re sending a love letter through the internet. You want it to be read only by your special someone, not intercepted by prying eyes. This is where cryptography, the magical guardian of our digital secrets, comes into play.
Cryptography transforms your readable love letter (plaintext) into a secret code (ciphertext) that only your special someone can decipher. It’s like having a secret language that only the two of you understand. This process is called encryption, and it ensures that your heartfelt words stay private.
Historically, cryptography began with simple tricks like Caesar’s substitution cipher. In World War II, it evolved with complex machines like the Enigma, which created nearly unbreakable codes. Today, advanced algorithms like RSA (Rivest-Shamir-Adleman) and AES (Advanced Encryption Standard) are the digital fortresses that protect our online activities.
Why is this important in our daily lives? Think about when you shop online or send a personal message. Cryptography is at work behind the scenes, ensuring your credit card details and private conversations remain secure. The padlock icon in your web browser’s address bar indicates a secure, encrypted connection (HTTPS), giving you peace of mind.
Even as technology advances and new threats emerge, cryptography continually adapts, much like a digital superhero. It’s the unsung hero that keeps our secrets safe, ensuring our personal and financial information stays out of the wrong hands.
So, next time you send a private message or make an online purchase, remember the invisible guardian working tirelessly to protect your digital life. Cryptography is more than just science; it’s the protector of our modern world.
